As demonstrated above, Timorese migrants in Dungannon and Cookstown have
encountered significant social, economic, and linguistic challenges in obtaining housing
and employment while establishing their lives in these locations. Concurrently,
within their local communities, they are actively developing networks of
solidarity and social interaction. Examining the processes by which Timorese
men and women established or re-established themselves in these small
Northern Irish towns provided insight into their agency in constructing
new lives and adapting to new environments despite the obstacles
they face.
