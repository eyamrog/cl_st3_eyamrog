Paresi (Glottocode: pare1272; latitude: -14.59, longitude: -57.41) is an indigenous
language spoken by approximately 3,000 individuals, who are distributed across
several communities in the State of Mato Grosso, Brazil, primarily
near the city of Cuiabá along the tributaries of the
Juruena River. The Enawene Nawe language (Glottocode: enaw1238; latitude: -12.43,
longitude: -58.98) is spoken by an estimated 1,000 individuals residing
in two communities, Halataikwa and Kolinakwa, within an Indigenous Territory
located near the cities of Juína (State of Mato Grosso)
and Vilhena (State of Rondônia).
