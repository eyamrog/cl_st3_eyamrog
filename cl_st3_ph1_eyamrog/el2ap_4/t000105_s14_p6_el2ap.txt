Residents in permanent households in the urban areas of the
cities of Ouro Preto and Mariana, aged 18 years or
older, were considered eligible for this study. According to data
from the 2010 demographic census, there were 44,569 and 33,902
inhabitants, distributed in 17,753 and 14,078 households, in Ouro Preto
and Mariana, respectively [12]. The protocols adopted and described below
were identical for both the municipalities.
