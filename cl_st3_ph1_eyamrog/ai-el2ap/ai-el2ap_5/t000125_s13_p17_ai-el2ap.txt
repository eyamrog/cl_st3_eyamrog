Figure 3 presents the percentage of available data for each
of the 35 indicators utilized across all four years examined
in this study. The indicators are as follows: 1 -
Bachelor graduation rate, 2 - Master graduation rate, 3 -
On-time bachelor graduation, 4 - On-time master graduation, 5 -
Citation rate, 6 - Absolute number of research publications, 7
- Size-normalized research publications, 8 - External research income, 9
- Art-related output, 10 - Top-cited publications, 11 - Interdisciplinary
publications, 12 - Postdoctoral positions, 13 - Strategic research partnerships,
14 - Professional publications, 15 - Co-publications with industrial partners,
16 - Income from private sources, 17 - Absolute number
of patents awarded, 18 - Size-normalized patents awarded, 19 -
Industry co-patents, 20 - Spinoffs, 21 - Publications cited in
patents, 22 - Income from continuous professional development, 23 -
Graduate companies, 24 - Foreign language bachelor programmes, 25 -
Foreign language master programmes, 26 - Student mobility, 27 -
International academic staff, 28 - International joint publications, 29 -
International doctorate degrees, 30 - Bachelor graduates employed in the
region, 31 - Master graduates employed in the region, 32
- Student internships in the region, 33 - Regional joint
publications, 34 - Income from regional sources, and 35 -
Strategic research partnerships in the region. The indicators “Graduating on
time (long first degree),” “Long first degree graduation rate,” “Open
Access Publications,” and “Regional Publications with Industrial Partners,” which were
used exclusively in 2020, are excluded from this analysis. Each
year from 2017 to 2020 is represented by a distinct
line in the figure: blue for 2017, red for 2018,
green for 2019, and purple for 2020.
