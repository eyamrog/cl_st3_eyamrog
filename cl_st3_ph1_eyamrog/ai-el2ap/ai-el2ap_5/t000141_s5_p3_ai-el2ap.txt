The strategies implemented by state and federal governments in Brazil
to address SARS-CoV-2 infection have been inconsistent, which has hindered
widespread adherence to World Health Organization recommendations, including hygiene practices,
respiratory etiquette, social distancing, social isolation, quarantine, and other restrictive
measures.(44,45) The intervention of the Brazilian Supreme Court played a
critical role in affirming the autonomy of states and municipalities
in public health decision-making, thereby counteracting certain controversial measures introduced
by the federal government.(46-48)
