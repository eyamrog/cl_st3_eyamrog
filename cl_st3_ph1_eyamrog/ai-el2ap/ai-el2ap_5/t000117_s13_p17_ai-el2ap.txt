The formation of the subject does not occur exclusively within
a specific setting such as the school, but rather across
various environments associated with different dimensions of human life, including
the family and other contexts within civil society. In this
regard, the article of the LDBEN aligns with the perspectives
presented in both the Faure (1973) and Delors (1998) reports,
as both advocate for the extension of educational processes beyond
the confines of the school to encompass all areas of
life. As stated by Faure et al. (1973, p. 243),
“Instead of delegating powers to a single structure, vertically hierarchised
and constituting a distinct body within society, all groups, associations,
trade unions, local collectivities and intermediate bodies should all take
up their share of educational responsibility [...]”.
