The first aspect concerns the relationship between the reports by
Edgar Faure (1973), Jacques Delors (1998), and the LDBEN 9.394/1996.
Although the law serves a regulatory function while the two
reports provide recommendations, analysis indicates that all three documents contribute
to a conceptualization of the subject as engaged in continuous
learning, extending beyond the confines of the traditional school setting.
Consequently, these documents contain statements that align with Noguera-Ramírez’s (2009)
definition of the learning society, characterizing individuals as being shaped
by the experience of lifelong learning under governmental influence.
