Data from 5,564 Brazilian municipalities were analyzed in this study.
The greatest reduction in medical appointments attributable to the pandemic
was observed in May 2020 (IRR = 0.27, 95% CI
[0.24–0.30]). For prenatal procedures, a 65% reduction was also recorded
in May (IRR = 0.35, 95% CI [0.32–0.38]). The most
substantial decreases in diabetes care occurred in April 2020 (IRR
= 0.24, 95% CI [0.11–0.53]) and May 2020 (IRR =
0.19, 95% CI [0.09–0.43]). Overall, from February to December 2020,
the pandemic had a significant impact on the total number
of procedures evaluated.
