Plants yellowish to golden, with leaves that are crowded, oblong-lanceolate
to lingulate, and possess a short-acuminate to apiculate apex with
a crenulate margin; inner basal cells are reddish-yellow, and the
costae are percurrent. This species was frequently observed in high
elevation grasslands during the study. It is rupicolous and terricolous
within the Atlantic Forest and has been recorded in the
states of Espírito Santo, Minas Gerais, Rio de Janeiro, São
Paulo, and Santa Catarina; it is endemic to Brazil.
