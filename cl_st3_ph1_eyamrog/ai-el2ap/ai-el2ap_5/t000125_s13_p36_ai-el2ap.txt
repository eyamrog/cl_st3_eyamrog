Following ESPM, which achieved an average score of 2.82 points
for the Dimensions, the State University of Campinas ranks second
with 2.05 points, maintaining the same score as in 2019.
It is currently the highest-ranked among the largest Brazilian universities.
The Federal University of Ceará occupies third place, trailing the
State University of Campinas by only 0.02 points. The University
of São Paulo is in fourth place, surpassing the 2.00-point
threshold with an average score of 1.85. UNESP São Paulo
State University has dropped to fifth place, primarily due to
both improvements by other institutions and its own decline, now
holding an average score of 1.76, which represents a significant
decrease of 0.66 points compared to 2019.
