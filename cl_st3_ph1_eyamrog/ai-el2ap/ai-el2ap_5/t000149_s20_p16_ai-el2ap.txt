At a general level, the highest proportion of toxic or
potentially toxic species is of exotic origin. Site-specific analysis indicates
that in the RZ and VP sites, the percentage of
exotic toxic species exceeded that of native species. In contrast,
at the PP and FE sites, native toxic species—including Asclepias
mellodora A.St.-Hil., Baccharis ulicina Hook. & Arn., Euphorbia collina, E.
serpens Kunth, E. hieronymi Subils, Clematis montevidensis Spreng., Nierembergia linariaefolia
Graham, Nicotiana noctiflora Hook., and Solanum triflorum Nutt.—accounted for a
higher percentage than exotic toxic species (Fig. 7).
