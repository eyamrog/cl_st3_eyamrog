The Wajãpi Indigenous Land (WIL) is located between the Jari
River basin to the west, the Amapari River basin to
the east, and the Oiapoque River basin to the north.
It was officially recognized by the Brazilian government in 1996
and encompasses an area of 6,070 km². The region is
characterized by dense tropical forest and rugged terrain, forming part
of the Tumucumaque Mountains complex. The Wajãpi population currently consists
of approximately 1,221 individuals residing in more than 80 villages
[15].
