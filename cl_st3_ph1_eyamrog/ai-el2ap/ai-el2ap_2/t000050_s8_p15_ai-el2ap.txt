The necessity for a more comprehensive and sensitive assessment of
this epidemiological context from ecological, cultural, and ethnic perspectives led
to a critical review of the objectives and methodologies employed
in both indigenous health services and epidemiological research. This review
also encompassed an epistemological reconsideration of how causality in health
and disease is understood, emphasizing its intrinsic connection to social
and ecological processes across various scales and dimensions of perception.
Consequently, obtaining data on the occurrence of ACL vectors was
considered essential.
