It is noteworthy that the only interviewee who did not
consider mayoral support important for a legislator’s election was a
second-term representative whose activities are concentrated in a specific policy
area. This legislator’s most significant local relationships are with other
actors, including bureaucrats in managerial positions, NGO presidents, and organizations
operating within the same policy domain. According to her adviser,
their office makes extensive use of social media to publicize
the legislator’s actions, indicating a deliberate strategy to minimize the
role of local politicians as intermediaries and to establish a
more direct connection with voters.
