Although most interviewees express confidence in the significance of these
alliances, they frequently describe them as uncertain and prone to
various disappointments. They report that mayors often deliver significantly fewer
votes than initially promised or anticipated by legislators. However, such
outcomes do not necessarily result in the dissolution or weakening
of the alliance; instead, factors such as loyalty, the strength
of the relationship, and a history of prior collaboration are
important in the restoration and maintenance of trust.
