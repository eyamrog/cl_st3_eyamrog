The municipality of Tapauá is situated on the banks of
the Purus River, with a population of 16,876 inhabitants and
a total area of 84,946 km² [11]. The primary economic
activities include agriculture, focusing on crops such as cassava, jute,
and beans, as well as extractivism of products such as
nuts, rubber, wood, copaiba oil, and andiroba. Over the past
ten years, livestock production has emerged as the predominant economic
sector.
