Of the studies reviewed, 20 (28.5%) addressed the use of
topical anesthetics for the management of malignant fungating wound (MFW)-related
pain. The study designs included non-systematic reviews (75%), case studies
(5%), randomized clinical trials (5%), guidelines (5%), prospective cohort studies
(5%), and surveys (5%). Nine studies (45%) provided specific dosages
and application protocols for various topical anesthetics, which are summarized
in Table 6. The remaining 11 studies (55%) referenced the
use of lidocaine, prilocaine, benzocaine, and a tricyclic anesthetic, but
did not specify the formulation or method of administration.
