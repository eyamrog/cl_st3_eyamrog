In the context of a complex reality in which confinement
serves as the primary prophylactic measure to prevent the spread
of the virus (2, 77), both educators and learners adopt
new adaptive behaviors and strategies to respond appropriately to the
situation, resulting in physiological changes associated with the outbreak. The
process of coping with COVID-19 requires cognitive and affective regulation
to manage the escalation of fears and insecurities that contribute
to psychological distress.
