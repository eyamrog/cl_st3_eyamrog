Among the university students surveyed, 21.7% reported spending between three
and four hours per day using their smartphones, 19.6% reported
more than six hours, and 16.9% reported between five and
six hours. Smartphone usage time is distributed relatively evenly between
academic activities (67.8%) and engagement with social networks (69.6%) (see
Figure 6). Regarding leisure activities conducted via smartphone, the most
common are taking photos or videos (92.9%) and listening to
music (74.9%). In contrast, activities such as watching videos related
to television and listening to the radio are less prevalent
on smartphones, with less than 10% of students engaging in
these activities through their devices. However, when considering these activities
in general, 71.2% of students watch television and 61% listen
to the radio, regardless of the device used.
