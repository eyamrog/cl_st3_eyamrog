Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a respiratory illness caused by
the novel coronavirus (2019-nCoV), which was first identified in Wuhan,
China, in December 2019 [1]. The rapid global dissemination of
this virus has posed significant challenges to public health systems
worldwide [2]. According to a report by the World Health
Organization (WHO) [2], as of April 30, 2020, the COVID-19
pandemic had resulted in 3,090,445 confirmed cases and more than
217,000 deaths, with common clinical manifestations including fever, dry cough,
and shortness of breath [3,4].
