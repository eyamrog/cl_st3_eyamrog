Although overall progress in meeting the agreements of the convention
has been limited, the issue remains under ongoing review due
to its global significance, particularly for developing countries. These countries
possess the highest levels of biodiversity, which are also the
most at risk. Latin America and the Caribbean are notable
for their exceptional natural resources compared to other regions, as
well as for the significant threats to the preservation of
these resources (León and Cárdenas, 2020).
