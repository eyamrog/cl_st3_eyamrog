Evidence indicates that remote work participation is higher among women
(15.88%), white individuals (14.26%), older workers, those residing in urban
areas (12.95%), and individuals employed in the service sector (16.8%).
Conversely, remote work participation is lower among men (7.56%), non-white
individuals (8.14%), younger workers, and those employed in agriculture (1.37%),
industry (3.9%), and retail (4.53%) (Figure 3).
