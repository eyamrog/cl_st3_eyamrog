While students often identify having a good advisor as the
most positive aspect of the USR experience, the converse is
also true, with negative experiences frequently attributed to poor advising.
This observation supports the argument that mimetic experiences do not
produce exact replicas but rather facilitate the recognition of models.
Consequently, the role of USR in the moral development of
researchers should not be assumed to be inherently positive. Drawing
on Adorno (1995), it can be argued that some individuals
suppress their mimetic impulses in favor of rationality, and, having
internalized this process, may feel justified in perpetuating similar forms
of coercion toward others. This tendency appears to be increasing
