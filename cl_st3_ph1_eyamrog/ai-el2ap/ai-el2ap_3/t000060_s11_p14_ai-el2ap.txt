As the scholar notes, questions and answers provide a clear
example—arguably the canonical one—of what Harvey Sacks termed a "first
pair part" and a "second pair part," which together constitute
a minimal dialogic unit. This unit consists of two sequential
utterances of related types, forming what is known as an
"adjacency pair." The first pair part establishes a "conditional relevance"
for the subsequent turn, meaning that whatever is produced in
the following slot will be evaluated in terms of its
appropriateness as a response. If no response is provided, the
resulting silence is itself treated as significant and is interpreted
