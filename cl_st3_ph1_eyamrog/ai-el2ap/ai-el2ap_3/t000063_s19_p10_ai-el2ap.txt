Among respondents who reported a change in interests as a
result of the pandemic, a higher proportion were female (23%)
compared to male (20%), more were studying in public schools
(25%) than in private schools (17%), and a greater percentage
had neither parent with a university education (25.7%) compared to
those whose both parents had attended university (17.2%). Additionally, two
out of the eleven adolescents (18%) who indicated that they
do not intend to pursue a career, representing 1.1% of
the total sample (N=983), reported that their decision was influenced
by the pandemic.
