During social interactions, speakers aim to maintain face in order
to facilitate smooth communication; this involves both safeguarding their own
face and demonstrating respect for the face of others. Preserving
negative face requires the protection of personal boundaries and privacy,
while preserving positive face involves the need for recognition and
appreciation by others. These objectives may conflict, as the desire
to maintain negative face, such as by asserting privacy, can
be at odds with others’ efforts to uphold positive face,
