Overall, the study protocols were predominantly classified as having a
low risk of bias across most assessed items. Only one
study was classified as having a high risk of bias,
while two studies had an unclear risk regarding the random
sequence generation item. Five studies were rated as unclear in
relation to allocation sequence concealment. With respect to blinding of
participants and researchers, three studies received an unclear classification. Twelve
studies demonstrated a low risk of bias in all assessed
items, indicating a higher level of methodological rigor.
