First, conducting a global investigation of the target discourse community
addressed the specific needs of the intended audience, namely students
from various regions of Brazil who are required to share
their research findings with peers worldwide. The diverse backgrounds of
these students have resulted in varying levels of experience with
academic literacy and publication, which could be effectively examined remotely.
This approach not only broadened the scope of the contextual
TSA but also optimized the use of time and resources
during course preparation.
