In journals edited in Brazil (n=396,293), Portuguese accounts for the
highest proportion of articles in the Social Sciences and in
Multidisciplinary Social Sciences and Humanities (85%). English-language articles constitute the
majority in Engineering and Technology (65.1%) and in the Natural
and Exact Sciences (54.5%). The proportion of articles in English
is also substantial in Medical and Health Sciences (44.4%) and
in Multidisciplinary fields (42.4%). In journals from Spanish-speaking countries, Spanish
is more prevalent than Portuguese. As a result, 8.4% of
articles in Multidisciplinary Social Sciences and Humanities journals and 7.9%
of those in Humanities journals are published in Spanish.
