The referees most frequently commented on contextual aspects of the
research, such as its originality, contribution to existing knowledge, and
the impact of its results, with these factors cited in
90% of rejections and 50–63% of recommendations for publication. Textual
issues, including the quality of cited work, clarity of the
methodology, and accuracy of data analysis, were the second most
commonly mentioned, appearing in 60–63% of critical comments and 57–60%
of positive evaluations. Linguistic issues, such as syntax, spelling, and
coherence, were the least frequently mentioned, followed by concerns related
to formatting and the publishing process.
