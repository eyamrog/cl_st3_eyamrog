Plant species and study site – Stem and leaf samples
for this study were collected from the neotropical liana species
Amphilophium curcigerum (L.) L.G.Lohmann (Bignoniaceae; common name: Monkey’s comb) at
the Forest Reserve of the Institute of Biosciences, University of
São Paulo (23° 33’S, 46° 43’W), São Paulo, Brazil. The
samples were immediately transported to the Plant Anatomy Laboratory of
the Institute of Biosciences, where they were maintained under controlled
environmental conditions (average temperature: 25.35 ± 0.03 °C; relative humidity:
55.23 ± 0.53%).
