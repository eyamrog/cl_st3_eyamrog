This section presents the collection of indigenous language materials held
at the Goeldi Museum (Belém, Brazil) as a representative example
of a language archive based in Brazil, detailing its origins,
development, and contents (§3.1). Subsequently, it offers a concise overview
of other digital language archiving initiatives in Brazil (§3.2), highlighting
the Museu do Índio as the primary example. Additionally, several
language documentation projects and their outcomes are described to illustrate
the broader need for documentation and archiving in the country,
and the potential role of regional archives is discussed.
