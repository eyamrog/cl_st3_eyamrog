In this context, fundraising is a key factor for policymakers
seeking to maintain competitiveness in global economic markets. Foreign direct
investment (FDI) serves as an important channel for attracting financial
capital to a region. Despite the aforementioned challenges, the Financial
Stability Report published by the Central Bank of the Republic
of Turkey (CBRT) in 2016 indicates that Turkey achieved notable
macroeconomic adjustments aimed at sustaining economic growth following the events
of July 2016. Based on this context, the present article
examines the impact of FDI inflows on the real effective
exchange rate in Turkey.
