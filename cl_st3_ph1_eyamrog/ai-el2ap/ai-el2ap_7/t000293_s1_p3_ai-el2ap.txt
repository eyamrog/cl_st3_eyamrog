The mean age of the patients was 58.2 years; the
majority were male (60.4%) and had a prior diagnosis of
cancer (85.7%). Computed tomography (CT) demonstrated typical findings in 28.6%
of cases, indeterminate findings in 24.2%, and atypical findings in
26.4%. Reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) testing was positive
for SARS-CoV-2 in 27.5% of patients. The sensitivity, specificity, and
accuracy of the diagnostic approach in the first scenario were
64.0%, 84.8%, and 79.1%, respectively, while in the second scenario
these values were 92.0%, 62.1%, and 70.3%, respectively.
