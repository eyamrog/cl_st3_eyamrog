The Pataxó indigenous people live in the extreme south of
the state of Bahia, in 36 villages, distributed over six
indigenous lands: Águas Belas, Aldeia Velha, Barra Velha, Imbiriba, Coroa
Vermelha and Mata Medonha - located in the municipal regions
of Santa Cruz Cabrália, Porto Seguro, Itamaraju and Prado. Aldeia
Velha, located in Arraial D'Ajuda, in the municipal region of
Porto Seguro, covers an area of 2010 hectares, 80% of
which is native forest, and a mangrove area of approximately
10 km². Data from the Indigenous Health Care Information System
(or SIASI) recorded, in 2010, 11,436 inhabitants in 19 of
these villages, with 928 inhabitants in Aldeia Velha11.
