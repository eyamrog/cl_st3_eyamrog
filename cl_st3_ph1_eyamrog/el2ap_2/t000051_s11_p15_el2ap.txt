The documents show that multiple national leaders were considering and
rejecting Central and Eastern European membership in NATO as of
early 1990 and through 1991, that discussions of NATO in
the context of German unification negotiations in 1990 were not
at all narrowly limited to the status of East German
territory, and that subsequent Soviet and Russian complaints about being
misled about NATO expansion were founded in written contemporaneous memcons
and telcons at the highest levels. (SAVRANSKAYA & BLANTON, 2017)
