Study area – The research was conducted in a riparian
forest located in Planaltina, Distrito Federal, Brazil. According to the
Köppen-Geiger climate classification, the region is classified as Aw, characterized
by two distinct seasons: a dry season and a wet
season (Alvares et al., 2013). The mean annual rainfall is
1,393 mm, with nearly all precipitation occurring during the wet
season, which extends from October to April (Malaquias et al.,
2010). The average annual temperature is 20.7°C. The predominant soil
type is clay Plintossolo (Plinthosols), as defined by the Brazilian
Soil Classification System (Embrapa, 2018).
