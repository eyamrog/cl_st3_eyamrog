Riparian forests in the study area are characterized by high
plant species diversity. Tree density ranges from approximately 1,300 to
1,900 individuals per hectare, and basal area varies between 32
and 38 m² per hectare (Nóbrega et al. 2001; Silva
Júnior 2004, 2005). Common species in these forests include Protium
spruceanum (Benth.) Engl. (Burseraceae), Copaifera langsdorffii Desf. (Fabaceae), Tapirira guianensis
Aubl. (Anacardiaceae), Inga alba (Sw.) Willd. (Fabaceae), Cheiloclinium cognatum (Miers)
A.C.Sm. (Celastraceae), Pseudolmedia guaranitica Hasler (Moraceae), Euterpe edulis Mart. (Arecaceae),
and Talauma ovata A. St.-Hill. (Magnoliaceae) (Nóbrega et al. 2001;
Silva Júnior 2004, 2005).
