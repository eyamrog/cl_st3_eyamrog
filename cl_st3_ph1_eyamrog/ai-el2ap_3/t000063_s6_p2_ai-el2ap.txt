The COVID-19 pandemic influenced the study intentions of 22% of
respondents, with a greater impact observed among those interested in
health care (28%), followed by individuals intending to pursue careers
in natural sciences, mathematics, and statistics (23%). The majority of
adolescents who expressed interest in health sciences and natural sciences
indicated that their primary motivation for selecting these fields was
a personal interest in the subject matter, while only 3%
reported that the pandemic directly inspired their choice.
