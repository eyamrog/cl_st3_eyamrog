The scientific and technical literature addressing the impacts of Covid-19
in Brazil expanded rapidly in late 2020 and early 2021,
encompassing a range of perspectives. Among the initial studies relevant
to the objective of this analysis, Mattei and Heinen (2020)
utilized data from February to April 2020 and found that
the first individuals to lose their jobs or whose employment
became unviable due to the pandemic were the underemployed, typically
occupying more flexible positions.
