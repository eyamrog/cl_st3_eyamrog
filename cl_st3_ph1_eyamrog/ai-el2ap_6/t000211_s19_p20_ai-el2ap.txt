The Vascular Surgery Unit of a tertiary hospital in Singapore
reallocated 25% of its staff to support frontline pandemic care.
This reallocation was implemented in response to the increased demand
for healthcare professionals across all specialties during the pandemic, as
well as the need to manage patients requiring surgical intervention
who also exhibited symptoms of SARS-CoV-2 infection. To maintain both
emergency and elective vascular surgery services, two to three surgeons
remained at Tan Tock Seng Hospital, while the remainder of
the team was assigned to the National Center for Infectious
