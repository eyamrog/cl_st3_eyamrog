Characterized by lanceolate and strongly falcate leaves with a denticulate
margin, this species exhibits inflated alar cells that are slightly
obliquely arranged, as well as leaf cells with papillae that
are either conspicuous or nearly imperceptible. It is corticolous and
epixylous within the Atlantic Forest and has been recorded in
the Brazilian states of Minas Gerais, Pernambuco, Paraná, Rio de
Janeiro, Rio Grande do Sul, Santa Catarina, and São Paulo.
The species is endemic to Brazil.
