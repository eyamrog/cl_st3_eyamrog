The documents developed in the state of Bahia were applicable
exclusively at the municipal level. Due to the high number
of municipalities and the resulting fragmentation and lack of coordination
among actions, municipal-level plans appear insufficient for the effective management
of public health epidemics.(14) The documents from Amapá, Maranhão, and
Pernambuco were not included in the analysis because they lacked
clear and formalized indicators to guide the decision-making process.
