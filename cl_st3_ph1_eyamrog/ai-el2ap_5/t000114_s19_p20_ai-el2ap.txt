The species is characterized by long, narrowly lanceolate, and recurved
leaves that are gradually acuminate, with 2–3 oblong-inflated alar cells
arranged obliquely. It is corticicolous and, in the examined samples,
also rupicolous, occurring in the Atlantic Forest and recorded in
the Brazilian states of Alagoas, Bahia, Ceará, Distrito Federal, Espírito
Santo, Goiás, Minas Gerais, Mato Grosso, Pará, Pernambuco, Paraná, Rio
de Janeiro, Rio Grande do Sul, Santa Catarina, and São
Paulo. The species is endemic to Brazil.
