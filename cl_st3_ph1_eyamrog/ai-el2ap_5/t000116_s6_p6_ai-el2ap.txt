It is important to note that the most recent change
to the PNAB occurred only recently; therefore, findings lacking statistical
significance for the period after 2017 should be interpreted with
caution. Several authors have argued that the new guidelines have
hindered the consolidation of the national model, primarily by relaxing
requirements related to the number of families served by each
team and the composition of these teams. Additionally, the revised
policy grants municipal governments the discretion to determine whether to
adhere to the Family Health Strategy (FHS) or to implement
alternative models of primary health care.
