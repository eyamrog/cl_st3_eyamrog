Affirmative policies are implemented to address the disadvantages experienced by
groups that have been subjected to discrimination, injustice, racism, or
exclusion. In the context of Special Education, students with gifted
characteristics are often overlooked by teachers, guardians, and society at
large. The intersection of being gifted, Black, and female results
in compounded invisibility within educational settings. This study examines the
factors contributing to the invisibility of gifted Black female students
in Brazilian schools. Descriptive statistical methods are applied to microdata
obtained from surveys, assessments, and examinations conducted by the National
Institute of Educational Studies and Research.
