The emergence of a new form of education is no
longer a future prospect but a current reality. Digital connectivity,
as noted by Sobe, is now regarded as a fundamental
component of the right to information, or as an integral
element of contemporary life. In the present context, where many
social groups experience social isolation, both teaching and learning have
become increasingly complex challenges for educators and educational institutions. Consequently,
two simultaneous developments have occurred within schools. First, there has
been significant investment in the deterritorialization of education, a process
that some experts refer to as the “demmuring of childhoods.”
