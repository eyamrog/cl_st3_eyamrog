ICT, as one of the main contemporary tools for human
interaction, should also be an object of discussion in CEAP
classes, once they start changing social systems, enabling them to
become more globalized, and, nonetheless, increase social and economic discrepancies.1
In education, after a long period of adaptation, however, the
use of ICT starts to bring good results. Thus, besides
being an object of discussion, their use for language course
design, implementation, and analysis tends to increase shortly.
