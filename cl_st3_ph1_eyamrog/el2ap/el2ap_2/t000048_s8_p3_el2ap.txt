These plants can size from a few centimeters up to
10 meters in height. Some of the characteristics of this
groups sort from a rhizomatous stem, that usually stores starch;
presence of pseudostem, rarely true stem; parallel-pinned leaf venation; terminal
or basal inflorescences, generally surrounded by colored bracts; complete and
bisexual flowers, only Musaceae presents unisexual flowers, 1-6 fertile stamens,
1-5 sterile stamens that can look like petals (staminodes); inferior
ovary with three locules, each one may have one or
more ovules; fleshy or dried fruits; seeds with aryl in
general (Costa et al. 2011).
