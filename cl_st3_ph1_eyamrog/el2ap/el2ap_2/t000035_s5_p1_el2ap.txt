The prevalence of edentulism among Brazil’s indigenous population is high8,9.
The rates of tooth loss and the need for dentures
in the Pataxó are higher than those of the wider
Brazilian population21,22. In the current study, dental caries and periodontal
disease were cited as the main cause of tooth loss,
aggravated by the socioeconomic issues faced by indigenous people and
the invasive characteristics of the public dentistry service20.
