The same analysis used for the health macro-regions makes the
association more evident. In fact, by aggregating cities in large
regions, the outlier cities - whose tests per thousand exceed
more than two standard deviations from the average and are
small-sized - have their effect on the total overall reduced.
Pearson's coefficient for correlation between the elapsed time of the
first notified case in the state of Minas Gerais and
rapid tests was -0.59; between the first notified case and
RT-PCR tests, it was -0.25; between the first notified case
and incidence rate, -0.70; and between the first notified case
and mortality rate, -0.71.
