In this setting, WHO declared COVID-19 a pandemic on March
11, 2020, and instituted essential measures for prevention and confrontation.
They included hand washing with soap and water whenever possible
and alcohol gel use in situations where access to water
and soap is not possible. They also recommended avoiding touching
the eyes, nose and mouth, and protecting people around them
when sneezing or coughing, with the adoption of a cough
etiquette, by using a flexed elbow or disposable handkerchief.8 Moreover,
the WHO indicated maintaining social distancing (minimum of one meter),
avoiding crowding and using a mask in case of flu
or infection by COVID-19, or if a health professional in
caring for suspected/infected patients.8
