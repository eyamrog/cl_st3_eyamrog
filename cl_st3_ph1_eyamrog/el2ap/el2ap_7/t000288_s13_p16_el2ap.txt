The rapid identification of COVID-19 cases is crucial for the
containment of the pandemic. However, it is still challenging due
to the lack of pathognomonic symptoms, coupled with the limited
capacity to perform specialized polymerase chain reaction (PCR) tests49 -
which also have limitations. The need to develop fast accurate
molecular diagnostics is mandatory to identify a large number of
infected patients and asymptomatic carriers, in order to prevent the
transmission of the virus and ensure proper conduct.50,51 Rapid tests
can facilitate elective care in the future since the risk
of contamination by SARS-CoV-2 would be ruled out. However, the
dentist can never neglect the existence of other diseases transmitted
by saliva and aerosol, such as hepatitis B, measles and
tuberculosis.52,53,54
