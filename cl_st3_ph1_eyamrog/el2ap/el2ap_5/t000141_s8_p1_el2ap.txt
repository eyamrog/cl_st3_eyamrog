In January 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared the
spread of SARS-CoV-2 as a global public health problem and,
two months later, announced the pandemic state.(1,2) These actions considered
the new coronavirus's pathogenicity, virulence, and the unavailability of effective
treatment.(3-5) In this context, governmental measures to mitigate transmission and
lethality, which are fundamental to contain the epidemic, have a
substantial global impact, but their scope is still uncertain.(6)
