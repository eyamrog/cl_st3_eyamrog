The scientific and technical literature on the impacts of Covid-19
in Brazil grew rapidly in late 2020 and early 2021,
with several perspectives being addressed. Among the first published studies
in line with the objective of this analysis, that of
Mattei and Heinen (2020), which used data from February to
April 2020, found that the first dismissed workers or those
with jobs that were not feasible in the face of
the pandemic were the underemployed, generally in more flexible occupations.
