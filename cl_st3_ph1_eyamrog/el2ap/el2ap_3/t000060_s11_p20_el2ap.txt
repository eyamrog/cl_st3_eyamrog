Despite recognizing that a two-part structure, particularly the adjacency pair
“question/answer”, is very frequent in conversation, a potential three-part structure
also occurs. The Birmingham Discourse Analysis model suggests a potential
three-part structure rather than a two-part one. Coulthard and Brazil
(1992, p. 66) argue that “all eliciting exchanges have the
potential of a three-part structure, while accepting that a two-part
realization may, and in the case of polar responses often
does, occur”. As observed in the general practitioner consultation below,
