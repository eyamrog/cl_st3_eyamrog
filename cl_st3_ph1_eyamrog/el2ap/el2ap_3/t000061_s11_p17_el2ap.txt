For Benjamin (1994), perceiving in things only the similarities already
perceived determines the impoverishment of the experience. In the text:
On the program of the coming philosophy (Benjamin, 2001), the
philosopher announces his project to build a concept of experience
capable of including what he called the experience of thought.
For him, the experience “[f]ormed less with isolated data and
rigorously fixed in the memory, than with accumulated data, and
often unconscious, that flow into the memory” (Benjamin, 1994: 103).
