Specifically, in Brazil, in terms of job losses, the impacts
were severe. Between the quarters from January to March and
April to June 2020, there was a drop of 8.9
million or 9.6% in the employed population, reaching the lowest
level of the historical series (IBGE/PNADC, 2020). In May, out
of the employed population of 84.4 million, 15.7 million or
18.6% were away from work due to social distancing; in
June, the figure was 11.8 million (14.2%), in July, 6.8
million (8.3%) and, in August, 4.1 million, representing 5.0% of
the employed persons.
