Asked, with two possible answers offered, about which context the
respondents considered to have developed more autonomy, 92,3% (24) said
that in USR, and 7,7% (2) said in regular classes.
Such percentages are consistent with the argument that creating opportunities
for a model does not mean encouraging passive behavior on
the part of students; on the contrary, the model conveys
some security for the formation of own thought and action.
