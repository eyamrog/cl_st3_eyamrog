During the decade under study, the region was affected by
the most severe crisis of the twentieth century, the Great
Depression. While various indicators of economic distress were evident in
the Ecuadorian economy, gross domestic product (GDP) nonetheless continued to
grow at a moderate average rate of 1.5% between 1929
and 1934 (Morillo 1996:687), with no recorded decline in any
year during the period of the crisis.
