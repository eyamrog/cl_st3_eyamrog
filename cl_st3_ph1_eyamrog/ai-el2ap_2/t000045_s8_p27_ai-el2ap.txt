However, principal-agent models of brokerage may not adequately capture brokerage
relationships in which both parties are elected officials seeking votes.
The core assumptions of these models—particularly the existence of conflicting
objectives between principal and agent (Gailmard, 2012) and informational asymmetry
favoring the agent—do not consistently apply to the interactions between
Brazilian legislators and their local brokers. As demonstrated in the
following sections, these relationships exhibit considerable variation, and multiple forms
of equilibrium are possible.
