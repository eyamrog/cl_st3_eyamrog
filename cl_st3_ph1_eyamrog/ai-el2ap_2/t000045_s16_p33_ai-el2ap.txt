We therefore argue that the party is not the primary
factor structuring brokerage networks, but rather one of several factors
that contribute to their activation. Furthermore, the interviews indicate that
political parties can function as veto points, contingent upon the
specific political configuration of the municipality. The strength of political
groups in the local context and the degree of polarization
present can transform parties into obstacles that hinder or prevent
the formation of alliances between local politicians and legislators.
