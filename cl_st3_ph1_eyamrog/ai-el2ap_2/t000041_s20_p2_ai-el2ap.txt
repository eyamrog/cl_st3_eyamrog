Eudicotyledons were the predominant group among angiosperms, comprising 47 families,
with Fabaceae (48 species), Euphorbiaceae (23 species), Convolvulaceae (21 species),
Malvaceae (19 species), and Asteraceae (15 species) being the most
represented. In contrast, monocotyledons were recorded in 11 families, primarily
represented by Poaceae (nine species), Bromeliaceae (six species), Commelinaceae (six
species), and Cyperaceae (five species), all of which were frequently
observed in the study area.
