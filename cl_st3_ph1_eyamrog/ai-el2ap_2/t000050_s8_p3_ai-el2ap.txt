Our dataset contains fields describing, for each individual sand fly
specimen, the following information: (i) taxonomic classification (including kingdom, phylum,
class, order, family, genus, specificEpithet, verbatimIdentification, infraspecificEpithet, scientificName, scientificNameAuthorship, and
taxonRank); (ii) collection details, such as the names of collectors
(recordedBy: Shimabukuro PHF; Stumpp RGAV; Medeiros MES; Alves DRC; Moreno
ES; Freire MP; Nascimento LOC), collection date, trapping method, trap
identification number, and collection site description (verbatimEventDate, eventTime, habitat, samplingProtocol);
(iii) geolocation data (stateProvince, county, locality, locationRemarks, verbatimLatitude, verbatimLongitude, decimalLatitude,
decimalLongitude, geodeticDatum); and (iv) catalogue reference data (otherCatalogNumbers). The dataset
is available through the Sistema de Informação sobre a Biodiversidade
Brasileira (SiBBr), an online platform that integrates data and information
on biodiversity and ecosystems, and serves as the Brazilian Node
of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF), an internationally recognized
resource for biological occurrence data [4]. The dataset has been
submitted to this platform and is publicly available for use
(fiocruz_suruwaha_01: https://doi.org/10.15468/28xvr7 and fiocruz_wajapi_01: https://doi.org/10.15468/gt29ub).
