In 1937, the implementation of a standardized salary was extended
to the entire private sector, with wage levels differentiated by
geographical region at 300, 350, and 400 pesos per month.
In the municipality of Santiago, minimum daily wages were set
at 12 pesos for temporary workers and 13 pesos for
permanent workers. As indicated in Tables 8 and 9, the
equivalent salary in US dollars ranged from 4.24 to 15.72.
For the subgroup comprising minors, older adults, and workers with
disabilities, the minimum wage was set at 8 pesos.
