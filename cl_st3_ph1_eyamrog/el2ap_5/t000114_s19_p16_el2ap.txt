Leaves rigid, imbricate when dry and erect when moist, lanceolate-linear,
acuminate. Characterized by pyriform and smooth apical lamellae cell, Peralta
and Yano (2010) consider this plant the biggest species in
the family. Terricolous in Atlantic Forest (montane) and Pampa, recorded
in the states of BA, ES, MG, PR, RJ, RS,
SC and SP; Brazilian endemic.
