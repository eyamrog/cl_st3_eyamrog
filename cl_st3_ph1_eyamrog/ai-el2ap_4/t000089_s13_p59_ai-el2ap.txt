In England, the development of Religious Education (RE) teaching in
voluntary controlled and community schools provides opportunities for mutual learning
and critical dialogue by enabling pupils to articulate ethical perspectives
informed by their individual faith experiences. As demonstrated by the
Birmingham and Cornwall syllabuses, an emphasis on the specific social
and historical contexts of religious practice within each region facilitates
a deeper understanding of the reasons, values, and concepts that
various religious traditions associate with spiritual practices and commitments. This
approach supports a contextualized analysis of teleological meanings and truth
claims as understood by different faith communities. Additionally, attention to
