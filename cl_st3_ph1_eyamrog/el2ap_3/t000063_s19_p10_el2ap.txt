Those who reported a change in interests as a result
of the pandemic were more female (23%) than men (20%),
more studying in public schools (25%) than in private schools
(17%), and those whose neither parents studied in universities (25.7%)
than those whose both parents studied (17.2%). Two respondents (18%)
out of the 11 adolescents (1.1% of N=983) that will
not study a career stated that they changed their study
intentions as a result of the pandemic.
