It is interesting to note in the answers the constant
use of a material aspect in the description of the
USR experience, which can be understood along the lines of
what Benjamin (1985) understands as the formation of mental images,
linked to the human tendency to read the world first
by the images. In this sense, the respondents used verbs
that refer to the senses, such as seeing and looking,
and words that materialize concepts, such as distancing, which characterize
mimetic learning as one that is experienced throughout the body
