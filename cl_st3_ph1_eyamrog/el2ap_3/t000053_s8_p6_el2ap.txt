Phlebotominae sand flies (Diptera, Psychodidae) are insects of great medical
interest since they can transmit diseases like leishmaniases, bartonellosis and
some arboviruses [1]. In the Americas, 547 sand fly species
have been recorded and, the majority in tropical areas, where
Amazon region shows the greatest diversity and richness for this
insects [2, 3]. Located in this region, the state of
Acre state exhibits a great richness of phlebotomine species, with
recent reports of new records and descriptions of new species
[2, 3, 4, 5, 6].
