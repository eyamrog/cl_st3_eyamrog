Regarding the monitoring of information flow, we consider the concept
of involvement elaborated by Chafe (1982, p. 47): “involvement includes
monitoring by the speaker of the communication channel which exists
with the listener, and attempts to make sure that the
channel is functioning well”. The speaker can employ certain strategies
that help the reaffirmation of what is being said by
calling listeners’ attention to a certain point of the subject
in progress. Therefore, well, you know, and I mean, among
other markers, are very appropriate for this kind of function.
